What is Project Zero Fin-vasion?

A three-phase project that involves conducting baseline research and data gathering to monitor and assess the current state of freshwater invasive species using GRIIS, working with riverside communities and local fisherfolk through discussions, seminars, and workshops to raise awareness on IAS, and formally proposing a budget increase for organizations such as DENR and BFAR for research and development on more effective and technologically advanced solutions for this problem.

Mission

Project Zero Fin-vasion is committed to protecting freshwater ecosystems from invasive alien species (IAS) through scientific research, community engagement, and policy advocacy. By fostering awareness, equipping local stakeholders, and pushing for sustainable and technologically advanced solutions, we aim to mitigate the impact of IAS and prevent further introduction, safeguard native biodiversity, and promote responsible resource management for the benefit of the present and future of humankind and environment.

Vision

This advocacy envisions a future where freshwater ecosystems in the Philippines thrive free from the threats of invasive species, ensuring ecological balance, sustainable fisheries, and improved livelihoods for local communities. Through proactive conservation efforts, technological advancements, and collaborative action, we strive to support the global mission of SDG 14 of preserving and protecting life below water for a healthier and more sustainable planet.
